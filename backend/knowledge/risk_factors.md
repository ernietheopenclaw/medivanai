# Risk Factors by Condition

## Skin Cancer
- UV exposure (cumulative and intermittent intense)
- Fair skin (Fitzpatrick I-II)
- Family/personal history of melanoma
- Multiple or atypical nevi (>50 common, >5 atypical)
- Immunosuppression
- Age >50 for non-melanoma skin cancers

## Diabetic Retinopathy
- Duration of diabetes (strongest predictor)
- Poor glycemic control (HbA1c >7%)
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
- Pregnancy
- Nephropathy/microalbuminuria
- Ethnicity (Hispanic, African American, South Asian at higher risk)

## Cardiovascular/Chest Findings
- Smoking
- Hypertension
- Diabetes
- Obesity
- Family history of heart disease
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Age >45 (men), >55 (women)
- Dyslipidemia

## Tuberculosis
- Close contact with active TB case
- Immigration from high-prevalence country
- HIV/immunosuppression
- Healthcare workers
- Incarceration
- Homelessness
- Substance use
