# Screening Guidelines

## Skin Cancer Screening (AAD/USPSTF)
- No universal screening recommendation (USPSTF I statement)
- AAD recommends annual full-body skin exam for high-risk individuals
- High risk: personal/family history, >50 moles, atypical moles, fair skin
- Monthly self-examination encouraged for all adults

## Diabetic Retinopathy Screening
- Type 1 DM: Annual dilated eye exam starting 5 years after diagnosis
- Type 2 DM: At diagnosis and annually thereafter
- Pregnancy with DM: Each trimester
- Can extend to every 2 years if no retinopathy on prior exams

## Chest X-ray Screening
- Not recommended for routine screening in asymptomatic adults
- Low-dose CT preferred for lung cancer screening in high-risk individuals (USPSTF)
- Indicated for: persistent cough, dyspnea, chest pain, suspected TB exposure
- Pre-operative screening per institutional protocols
